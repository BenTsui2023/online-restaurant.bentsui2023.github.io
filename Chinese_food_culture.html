The Eight Great Cuisines of China

Because of the different environment and location of China, Chinese cuisine is diverse and the style is different on different cities in China. Based on the regionality of Chinese cuisine, Chinese foods are separated into eight major Chinese cuisines, which is called the eight great cuisines.

Summary of Eight Great Cuisines of China

  1. Guangdong Cuisine
    • Usually use braising and stewing techniques
    • Popular ingredients are seafood and rice
  2. Sichuan Cuisine
    • Usually Spicy and bold techniques
    • Popular ingredients are chili, garlic and ginger
  3. Jiangsu Cuisine
    • Usually colorful and fresh
    • Use moderately salt and focus on health
  4. Zhejiang Cuisine
    • Usually fresh and light
    • Use fresh seafood and freshwater fish
  5. Fujian/Min Cuisine
    • Usually sweet and sour
    • Use ingredients from the sea and the mountains
  6. Hunan Cuisine
    • Usually spicy and sour
    • Use cooking techniques such as stir-frying, steaming and smoking
  7. Anhui Cuisine
    • less emphasis on seafood
    • Use many herbs and vegetables
  8. Shandong Cuisine
    • Usually salty and crispy
    • Tend to preserve the original taste of the seafood
