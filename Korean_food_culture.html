Korean Food Culture

Features of Korean Food Culture

Rice, vegetables, seafood and meats are the main ingredients of Korean cuisine. Like Chinese cuisine, Korean cuisine seldom uae dairy. Also, they eat fermented foods such as kimchi, doenjang and ganjangas as side dishes called banchan every meal.
As most of the banchan is made with fermentation for flavor and preservation, many side dishes are salty, and spicy in taste and this is one of the reason why Korean food is well known for being spicy.

Besides, Korean believes that food and medicine are grown from the same root. They eat medicinal food which includes ginseng, chicken and beef bone soups etc. regularly to restore physical stamina.

The Culture of Kimchi

Kimchi is the most famous Korean side dish and listed as UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage Item. Kimchi is an essential part of every Korean meal and also treated as a health food foor Korean because of its alkalinity.

Common soups or stew in Korean

In Korean, people usually start with a soup or stew before they eat the first dish. The following table shows the common soups or stew in Korean.

Common soups or stew Description
Doenjang-guk A soup made with soybean paste and ingredients, such as vegetables, meat and seafood
Tteokguk A sliced rice cake soup
Gomguk A beef bone soup simmered with low flame
Naengguk A soup with sour and sweet taste made with onions, garlic and seaweed
Miyeok-guk A non-spicy seaweed soup
Malgeunguk A soup made with red bean paste, meat, seafood and vegetables
